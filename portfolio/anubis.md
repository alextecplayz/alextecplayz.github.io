### What is Project Anubis?

Project Anubis, or better known by its intended product name, AEDIS, is a mobile and PC tower defense/city builder game I worked on for a little while between August 23 and September 14, 2023 to test out the concept. The game is currently on hold, and its future is unclear. It might be developed again if there's any interest in this idea.

**2026 update: AEDIS will be moved to Godot Engine and a small demo may be released by the end of 2026**

#### Decor buildings

Buildings such as the bazaar, tall buildings that have no defense measures are meant to serve as decor, and would be used in the city-building mode, in-between rounds, to boost your income.

AEDIS is supposed to integrate both simple tower defense and city building mechanics, but with somewhat of a twist: the game is round-based, and takes place in settlements all over Ancient Egypt, such as Memphis, Heliopolis, other cities along the Nile such as Naqada, Luxor, Herakleopolis and Abydos. You, the player, are an architect employed by the pharaoh, and you've been tasked to fortify and expand existing cities or settlements along the Nile, equip them with defense capabilities while also having an effect on both the local and overall economy of Egypt, as you make your way to Memphis, to greet the pharaoh.

One round, you build defense structures such as towers and traps, which would usually occur during the night, while in the next round, which would usually occur during the day - as your defenses structures fend off any attackers - would build buildings that could serve as decorations or have an economic impact, such as increasing your income over the course of the remaining rounds, paths, residential buildings, fountains, temples, other facilities, etc. You would alternate between these rounds until you reach round 40, after which you would complete the level.

You'd have three main objectives to protect, similar to Clash of Clans: the House of Writings (similar to a town hall), the Temple or an important building specific to that location, and the "jewerly houses", which are essentially banks. If all 3 objectives are failed, you fail the level. You can complete the level with two objectives failed, however.

As you progress, you're supposed to collect certain sacred objects (sacra), which would be carried with you as you reach Cairo, because here is where things get interesting: by collecting these sacred objects and bringing them with you, you've been helping the pharaoh to 'revive' an ancient god - Ra.

Over the course of the game, you would have some text-based character interactions in cutscenes between you, the player/architect, and emissaries of the pharaoh, which would collect these sacred objects and deliver them to the pharaoh. In the background of some of these cutscenes, you would see the pyramid of the ancient god, in multiple stages - a broken down version, a version that is being restored, and a version that is restored, and ready to welcome the new god. Additionally, during a special event in a Halloween-themed update, the restoration version of the pyramid would have green lights coming out from the inside, and the golden tip would be bright green.

#### Defense buildings

Buildings such as the Obelisk tower, Archery Tower, Ankh Tower, and of course, the Cactus.

I took some creative liberties with the designs of some of these towers, as obviously, there are no mystic towers in real life, or poison towers nowadays. They all have distinct colours and/or designs to help the player differentiate one building from another, providing some 'freshness' and helping with accessibility.

The towers would be priced differently, as they would all have different 'tiers'. Additionally, I was considering the idea of tower upgrades, that would grant both cosmetic and gameplay changes to each building. Unfortunately, I didn't make any voxel towers to illustrate such changes.

#### Other information

AEDIS was supposed to be a simple tower defense/city-building game, but the plans and scope grew larger over time.

Ideas such as: what if AEDIS were to take place in multiple eras and locations, and to have unique architecture for each location? This changed AEDIS from a simple Ancient Egyptian tower defense game into a 'collection' of locations, that would grow over time with additional updates, including places such as Ancient Rome, Ancient Greece, the Aztec jungle, Atlantis, the Babylonian Empire, feudal Japan, and a Volcano.

Ultimately, Project Anubis is still on the table, and is a viable game that I would like to continue developing, but had to halt it due to other projects such as PRISONIA and the Sapphire Engine being prioritized. If you think you might have some ideas for this game, or want to see something included, don't hesitate to tell me on Mastodon, I'm always open to suggestions for all of my projects.
