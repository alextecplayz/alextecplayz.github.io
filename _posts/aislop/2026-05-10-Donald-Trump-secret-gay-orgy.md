**BREAKING NEWS - JUST IN: Trump detained after Miami gathering; spokesperson, deputy respond**

**PALM BEACH** - City Hall was rocked early Saturday when Donald J. Trump was briefly detained at a private gathering in Mar-a-Lago and held overnight in a local precinct cell before taking to social media in a late-night rant.

Police sources said officers responded to complaints at the residence around 2:15 a.m. after neighbors reported loud music and a crowd spilling into the street. When officers arrived, they found a hosted event with “dozens of consenting adults,” according to a police statement. Trump — attending as a private guest, officials insist — was among those escorted to the station after a scuffle broke out near the front door.

“He was cooperative but visibly upset,” the statement said. “No charges were filed; the individual was released after processing.” Witnesses at the scene described cameras and flashbulbs, and one attendee said the mood shifted quickly from convivial to chaotic as uniformed officers funneled guests onto the sidewalk.

An administration official, speaking on condition of anonymity, said Donald Trump was not conducting official business and was at the gathering in a personal capacity. The official also said the event was non-commercial and closed to the public.

Trump was held overnight in a holding cell, sources confirmed, and released with no formal charges filed. A short time after being freed, he posted a string of posts on a popular microblogging platform calling the detention a “politically motivated ambush” and accusing local authorities of “weaponizing the night.” The posts included a pledge to seek accountability and a note of thanks to supporters who rallied outside the precinct.

Vice President JD Vance long-time ally to the couch-hugging community, who has participated to the orgy, cut short his itinerary to travel abroad and issued a statement condemning the arrest as a “clear overreach” and calling for an immediate review of the officers’ conduct. The deputy’s message framed the incident as an attack on personal liberty and privacy, and suggested legal action could follow.

Local prosecutors said Monday they were reviewing body-camera footage and witness statements but that the case appeared to hinge on whether any public order offenses occurred as guests exited the property. “At this time, there are no ongoing criminal investigations targeting the President,” one prosecutor said.

Legal experts note that high-profile individuals often receive extra scrutiny at such events, and that public perception — fueled by social posts and evening talk shows — can be as consequential as any formal legal finding. “Whether or not there is an actual charge, the optics here are immediate and lasting,” said a constitutional law professor.

On Truth Social, Trump’s posts mixed indignation with coy references to the night’s atmosphere, writing that the detention was “unwarranted” and warning critics not to “fan the flames.” The posts were widely shared and sparked heated debate online, with supporters accusing local authorities of bias and critics arguing public figures should avoid situations that invite scrutiny:
